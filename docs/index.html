Features

A planner that behaves like a real daily page.

Daymark is not another endless to‑do list. It’s a structured daily planning system where each day has its own tasks, notes, and decisions—just like a physical planner.

Daily page metaphor
Each day gets its own space.

Open Daymark and you’re on today’s page. Yesterday and tomorrow are a swipe away. Tasks and notes stay anchored to the day they belong to, so your history and plans are always clear.

Focus on your priorities
A/B/C priorities, built in.

Mark tasks as A (must do), B (should do), or C (nice to do). Add statuses like Completed, Delegated, or Forwarded to reflect what actually happened—not just what you hoped to do.

Intentional forwarding
Unfinished doesn’t mean forgotten.

When a task doesn’t get done, you choose whether to forward it to tomorrow. That small act of intention keeps your list honest and your commitments visible.

Linked notes
Notes that live with your tasks.

Capture meeting notes, ideas, and reflections on the same page as your tasks. Reference A‑tasks, decisions, and follow‑ups so you always know why something is on your list.

Focused design
No clutter. No ads. Just planning.

Daymark is intentionally minimal. No gamification, no inbox of distractions—just a calm, focused workspace for planning your day.

Built for repetition
A daily ritual, not a one‑off tool.

Daymark is designed to be opened every morning and every evening. The more you use it, the more valuable your history, habits, and decisions become.

Your Daily Planning Ritual

A proven system, translated into a modern app.

For decades, paper-based planners have helped people manage their time with intention. Daymark brings that structure into a digital daily planner—without losing the simplicity that made it work.

“Most apps give you more lists. Daymark lets decide what actually matters today.”

How Daymark thinks about planning
  • A/B/C priorities: Classify tasks by importance, not just urgency, so your day reflects your real priorities.
  • Daily pages: Treat each day as a fresh page with its own commitments, notes, and outcomes.
  • Forwarding tasks: When something moves, it moves with intention—not by accident.
  • Reflection and review: Use notes to capture what happened, what changed, and what needs to carry forward.
How it works

A simple daily workflow you can actually stick to.

Daymark is designed around a repeatable daily ritual: plan in the morning, execute during the day, review and forward in the evening. No complex setup. No endless configuration.

  • Morning: Open today’s page, list your tasks, assign A/B/C priorities, and capture any key notes.
  • During the day: Mark tasks as Completed, Delegated, or Forwarded as reality unfolds.
  • Evening: Review what happened, add notes, and intentionally forward any unfinished tasks to tomorrow.
Designed for consistency

Because each day is its own page, your history becomes a narrative of what you chose to do—and what you chose not to do. That makes Daymark more than a checklist. It becomes a record of your decisions.
